GEO vs SEO: what actually changes (and what stays the same)

Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) is the practice of optimizing a website to be cited inside AI-generated answers — in ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini and Google AI Overviews. Classic SEO optimizes for a ranked list of links a human scrolls; GEO optimizes for the single answer an assistant composes. That one-sentence difference changes surprisingly much — and leaves more intact than the hype suggests.

What stays the same

  • Technical health still rules. A site that can't be crawled, loads slowly or renders everything through JavaScript is invisible to both Google's classic index and AI crawlers. Every hour invested here pays twice.
  • Quality content still wins. E-E-A-T — experience, expertise, authoritativeness, trust — is exactly what language models are tuned to prefer when picking sources.
  • Authority is still earned elsewhere. Backlinks taught Google to trust you; mentions across directories, reviews and press teach AI models the same thing.

What changes

  • The unit of victory. SEO wins a position; GEO wins a citation. An AI answer names 3–5 sources — there is no "page two" to survive on.
  • The reader. SEO copy is written for a human skimming a page. GEO content must also parse cleanly for a machine assembling an answer: direct definitions, concrete numbers, question-shaped headings.
  • The infrastructure. New layers appear: AI-crawler access in robots.txt (GPTBot, PerplexityBot, Google-Extended), llms.txt, and Schema.org markup that lets a model state facts about you with confidence.
  • The measurement. Rankings and clicks become mentions, citations and "was the information about us correct?" — tracked by asking the assistants themselves, month after month.

Do you need both?

Yes — and they are not in conflict. Everything GEO adds follows search-engine guidelines, so a GEO-optimized site tends to rank better, not worse. The practical order: fix the SEO foundation, then add the AI layers on top. Skipping the foundation to chase citations is building the roof first.

The myth worth killing

"AI search is too small to matter yet." The share of buying journeys that start with an assistant grows every quarter, and the businesses cited today are accumulating an advantage that gets more expensive to displace later. Positions can be re-taken; a model's learned preference for your competitor is much stickier. Being early is the whole game — run the 5-minute test to see where you stand.
